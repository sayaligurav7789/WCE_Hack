According to the textbook, Gestalt principles are a set of principles that describe how we organize sensory stimuli into meaningful perceptions. Some of the key Gestalt principles mentioned in the textbook include: 1. Figure-ground relationship: We tend to segment our visual world into figure (the object or person that is the focus of the visual field) and ground (the background). 2. Proximity: Things that are close to one another tend to be grouped together. 3. Similarity: Things that are alike tend to be grouped together. These principles help us understand how we perceive and interpret sensory information, and how our perceptions can be influenced by various factors such as our personalities, experiences, and expectations.
