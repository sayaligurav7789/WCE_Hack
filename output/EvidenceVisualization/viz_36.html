The challenges of cross-cultural psychology include the limitations of Eurocentric therapies, which may not be applicable to other cultures. For example, a counselor whose treatment focuses on individual decision making may be ineffective at helping a Chinese client with a collectivist approach to problem solving. Additionally, cultural differences in values, beliefs, and norms can affect the assessment, diagnosis, and treatment of mental health issues. Furthermore, cultural biases and stereotypes can influence the development of psychological theories and interventions, leading to a lack of cultural competence. To address these challenges, multicultural counseling and therapy aim to offer a helping role and process that uses modalities and defines goals consistent with the life experiences and cultural values of clients.
